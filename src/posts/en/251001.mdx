*The Mavic*, a collection by the newly formed progressive rock band Purgatorio—centered around CHIYOJI of Second Waltz and bassoonist Moja—will be released digitally on September 22.

The six-track release combines sounds evoking Canterbury bells and the passion of a Neapolitan sirocco with the members’ wide-ranging musical interests. The result is eclectic, dense progressive music.

“Hato to Olive” began with a melody inspired by AREA, but its direction changed during production. Saxophone, synthesizer, and piano create a refreshing summer atmosphere in the first half. From the middle onward, the guitar and bass turn slightly ominous and the entire sound changes dynamically. The finished track freely and naturally blends the members’ diverse tastes.

The title track, “The Mavic,” is a long science-fiction-inspired progressive piece based on historical footage of the Roman Republic. It depicts elite soldiers under Scipio fighting Hannibal’s troops, who wield modern drone technology. The track combines a strong narrative with powerful performances.

In this way, *The Mavic* is a unique collection that fully reflects the members’ varied musical roots and original ideas.
